Budgeting Basics

A budget helps you control your money instead of wondering where it went. The key is spending less than you earn and planning for both needs and wants.

Simple Budget Formula

  • 50% for Needs: Rent, food, utilities, transportation
  • 30% for Wants: Entertainment, dining out, hobbies
  • 20% for Savings: Emergency fund and future goals

Track your spending for one month to see where your money actually goes, then adjust to fit this formula.

Building Your Savings

Saving money protects you from emergencies and helps you reach your goals. Start small but start today.

Emergency Fund Priority

Your first goal should be saving $1,000 for emergencies. This prevents you from going into debt when unexpected expenses happen.

Easy Saving Strategies

  1. Automate transfers to your savings account
  2. Save any extra money like gifts or tax refunds
  3. Use apps that round up purchases and save the difference
  4. Try the 52-week challenge: save $1 in week 1, $2 in week 2, etc.

Managing Debt Wisely

Not all debt is bad, but managing it properly is crucial for your financial health.

Good Debt vs Bad Debt

  • Good Debt: Student loans, mortgages (help build your future)
  • Bad Debt: Credit cards, payday loans (high interest, no lasting benefit)

Debt Payoff Strategy

Always make minimum payments on time. Put any extra money toward either your smallest debt (for motivation) or highest interest rate debt (to save money).

Quick Tips

Money-Saving Ideas

  • Cook at home instead of eating out
  • Use student discounts everywhere
  • Cancel subscriptions you don't use
  • Use the minimum possible cost on expenses

Financial Goals

Start with $1,000 emergency fund, then work toward 3-6 months of expenses saved.
